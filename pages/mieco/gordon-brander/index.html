I’m building Noosphere, a worldwide decentralized protocol for networked thought. Think Notion or Roam, but massively multiplayer, and distributed over IPFS. Users can connect and share knowledge across a vast distributed graph that stores ideas like your brain does.

On Noosphere, users own and control their data, through secure local-first data containers called Spheres. Your sphere belongs to you, and is signed with a key only you control. Spheres can be linked together, and synced to IPFS, a peer-to-peer network.

My aspiration is for Noosphere to be a decentralized, resilient, user-owned repository for human thought.

Our entire protocol stack is open source, and we love to have new contributors. Most of it is written in Rust. https://github.com/subconsciousnetwork/noosphere

I’m also interested in collaborating with plugin authors for tools like Roam, Obsidian, LogSeq. Noosphere is designed to be a shared data repository that multiple apps can interoperate over. We’re interested in getting multiple “tools for thought” talking over Noosphere’s distributed thought graph.
